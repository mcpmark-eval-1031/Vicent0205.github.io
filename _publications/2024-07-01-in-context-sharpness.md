This paper investigates the inner representations of large language models during generation and discovers that the **sharpness of in-context token distributions** serves as a reliable alert signal for detecting potential hallucinations. Leveraging this insight, a training-free hallucination mitigation strategy is proposed that significantly improves the factual accuracy of LLM outputs without any parameter updates.
