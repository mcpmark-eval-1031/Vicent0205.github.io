This paper investigates the internal geometry of large language models with respect to truthfulness. We discover a **universal truthfulness hyperplane** in the LLM representation space that robustly separates truthful from untruthful statements across diverse topics, formats, and model families. This geometric structure enables lightweight linear probing classifiers for truthfulness detection with strong cross-topic and cross-model generalization.

**Authors:** **Junteng Liu**, Shiqi Chen, Yu Cheng, Junxian He
